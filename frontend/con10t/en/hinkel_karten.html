The topographic maps

The archive of Friedrich W. Hinkel contains over 540 topographic maps in differing formats and materials illustrating the entire spectrum of topographic information available for the Sudan. The cartographic material belongs to several editions of topographic maps - including official map sheets of the Sudan Survey Department as well as calendar sheets – spanning the entire area of the Sudan including the Republic of South Sudan. Due to its composition, the archive's map collection represents an important source of information for any kind of scientific research regarding the ancient or modern Sudan.

The maps contain a multitude of annotations regarding archaeological sites made by Friedrich W. Hinkel in preparation for his publication series “Archaeological Map of the Sudan“ and served as the basis for his published hand-drawn maps. Within the iDAI.world the maps were structured according to the AMS-system and integrated into iDAI.objects. By virtue of their geographic specifications the maps can also be accessed via the iDAI.gazetteer. The copyright for the hand-drawn annotations by Dr. Hinkel rests with the DAI.
