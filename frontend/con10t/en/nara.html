The aim of the ‘North African Research Archive’ (NARA) is to provide a platform for archaeological cultural heritage from North Africa, in particular the Maghreb states of Tunisia, Algeria, Libya and Morocco.

Bequest F. Rakob, Siga mausoleum
Section, edited, Film_0603
(D-DAI-ROM-RAK-09342)

Research on archaeological cultural heritage in this region has a relatively short history. The first excavations were conducted in the late 19th century in French North Africa. They primarily studied individual finds considered important at the time that were discovered at sites rich in history, such as Carthage. Systematic and extensively documented excavations only began in the 1950s in cooperation with international partners. The NARA project focuses on pre- and post-mortem bequests from researchers who studied North Africa from the mid-20th century onwards. These collections from the researchers’ personal possessions were bequeathed to the German Archaeological Institute (DAI) for research purposes during the researchers’ lifetimes or after their passing.

The holdings primarily consist of photographs (photographic negatives, slides, etc.) as well as written materials (excavation diaries, letters and other documents/notes) and drawings (find drawings, plans and sketches). Indexing these materials involves three main steps: at least roughly drawing up inventories of the holdings (and ideally making a preliminary selection of the holdings to be digitized), the actual digitizing itself, and then indexing the content of the materials in an archive. This third step will be undertaken using the iDAI.world, in which the digital objects will be archived together with the most important associated data to ensure their long-term preservation and to make them available to the public. First, the materials are entered in the iDAI.objects/Arachne database and the iDAI.gazetteer topography database; other iDAI.world databases, such as iDAI.bookbrowser, will also be linked.

In 2015, work began on drawing up inventories of the materials from Friedrich Rakob’s bequest and digitizing them. Archival indexing of these materials started in 2017. Friedrich Rakob was an architectural historian who led the DAI’s major excavations in Carthage and Chimtou/Simitthus in Northern Tunisia from 1974 to 1996. Once this subproject has been completed, other bequests relating to research in North Africa (such as bequests from Friedrich Wilhelm Deichmann, Christa von Hees-Landwehr and Josef Röder) will be digitized, indexed and integrated in NARA.

The ultimate objective is to link NARA with the ‘North African Heritage Archives Network’ (NAHAN). This multi-institute platform also aims to make scholarly materials from archaeological excavations in North Africa available. The platform was initiated in 2016 by European and North African institutions and is currently under construction. Its structure is based on the structure of the iDAI.objects/Arachne database.
